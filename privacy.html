Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

What LensLog Collects

When you publish a photo, LensLog stores the image, title, location, camera, lens, ISO, aperture, shutter speed, style, story, display name, submission time, and a protected technical record used for abuse prevention. If you add a Gmail ID in your profile, it is used to connect your local profile with your submissions and is not shown in the public feed unless you type it into a public field yourself.

Browser Profile Data

Your profile name, Gmail ID, favorite camera, home location, likes, and saved preferences are stored in your browser so the site feels personal when you return.

Photo Metadata

The upload flow prepares photos in the browser before publishing. This reduces file size and strips most hidden image metadata during normal uploads, but you should still avoid sharing sensitive information in public titles, locations, stories, or photos.

How Data Is Used

LensLog uses submitted data to show the public photo feed, photographer profiles, search/filter results, moderation reports, and basic app health information. LensLog does not sell submitted profile or photo data.

Moderation

Visitors can report public photos for review. Reported photos may be hidden or removed to protect the community.

Deletion Requests

To request removal of a submitted photo, contact the LensLog owner through the place where the LensLog link was shared and include the title, location, and approximate submission time.

Hosting

LensLog is hosted on Render. Hosting providers may process technical data such as IP addresses, request times, browser details, and server logs to operate the service.

Contact

For privacy or removal requests, contact the site owner through the Instagram, GitHub, or other profile where LensLog was shared.
